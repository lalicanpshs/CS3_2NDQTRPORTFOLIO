History of Ice Cream

Ice cream has come a long way from its earliest beginnings. It first appeared in China during the Tang dynasty, where milk was mixed with rice and chilled using snow. Over time, this idea spread to Europe, and by the 16th century, sweetened frozen dairy or "ices" became popular, later evolving into sorbets. As centuries passed, the first hand-cranked freezer was invented in the early 19th century, giving ice cream a smoother texture and making it easier to produce.

Soon after, factories in New York and Boston began mass-producing ice cream during the mid-1800s, marking the start of its industrial rise. With the invention of mechanical refrigeration, ice cream could now be stored and enjoyed year-round, turning it from a luxury to an everyday delight. Finally, innovations like the ice cream cone, introduced at the 1904 St. Louis World's Fair, made it more accessible and fun to eat. Overall, what began as a frozen delicacy for the few has transformed into one of the world's most loved desserts.
